- **Stddev of Δ:** ~2.9.
- **Axes with consistent drift:**
  - **L (Layout)** — recorded scores typically +0.7 above re-scores. Likely cause: picker awarding L3 to chrome and infra surfaces that don't meet the "spread that could be photographed and printed" bar.

The two largest negatives (sitechrome, journal-SEO) are both *foundation/infra* ships — strong signal that the rubric needs an infra-rescue clause.

## Rubric clarifications recommended

1. **Foundation / infra ships have a ceiling.** Add to the rubric:
   *"A ship that adds no perceptible type, motion, layout, interaction,
   or a11y surface — only routing, build-time output, or scaffolding
   for future surfaces to consume — caps at total 8. The distinctiveness
   it enables is counted on the *consumer* ship, not the foundation."*
   This would have correctly banded sitechrome (4) and journal-SEO (7).

2. **Axis L band 3 needs the "photographed-and-printed" test enforced.**
   L3 currently reads *"A spread that could be photographed and printed."*
   The picker is awarding L3 to sticky chrome and form pages. Recommend
   tightening to: *"A spread that could be photographed and printed
   AS AN EDITORIAL PAGE — chrome, forms, and lists default to L2 even
   when they use hairlines + marginalia. L3 requires the surface itself
   to read as a designed page."*

3. **Axis M band 2 needs a "motion present" precondition.**
   M2 currently reads *"Scroll-bound parallax, splittext stagger,
   mix-blend cursor — system-level motion."* The picker awarded M3 to
   a no-motion SEO ship. Recommend prefixing: *"M ≥ 2 requires the
   ship itself to introduce or modify motion. Inheriting existing
   reveals/parallax from primitives the ship consumes does not raise M
   above 1."*
